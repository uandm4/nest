story
Favn, meaning ‘embrace’ in Danish, is a collaboration between artist Jaime Hayón and Fritz Hansen.
The Favn Sofa is a fusion of Danish design and Hayón’s Spanish heritage. “I wanted to create a form that embraces you, something really organic, that’s why we named it FAVN” says the designer. Compact and comfortable, the Favn is great for smaller abodes or as a second sofa.
Dimensions
Height: 88cm x Width: 221cm x Depth: 93cm
Seat height: 42cm
Armrest height: 60cm
Materials & finishes
Three seater sofa upholstered in a variety of different Kvadrat fabrics. Each colourway contains three different fabrics, one for the shell, one for the back and seat cushions, and a third for the decorative cushions. Kvadrat Steelcut 2 (90% new wool worsted 10% nylon), Kvadrat Canvas 2 (90% new wool worsted 10% nylon), Kvadrat Divina MD (100% new wool), Kvadrat Hallingdal 65 (70% new wool 30% viscose), Kvadrat Divina Melange 3 (100% new wool), Kvadrat Remix 3 (90% new wool worsted 10% nylon), Kvadrat Divina Melange 3 (100% new wool).
The upholstery of the sofa’s various components can be extensively customised. Call us on 0044 114 243 3000 or email us at info@nest.co.uk for assistance from one of our team.

Jaime Hayon
Spanish artist and designer Jaime Hayon was born in Madrid in 1974. He trained as an industrial designer in Madrid and Paris. In 1997, he began working as a researcher at Fabrica, Benetton Group's communication and research centre in Treviso, Italy. 2004 saw the start of Hayon's individual career and he has kept himself busy with a spectrum of eclectic projects. From toys, to modern furniture and interior design as well as artistic installations, his boldness has been to transcend the borders of the often separated worlds of art and design, comfortably bridging the two whilst embedding them with his own unique style.

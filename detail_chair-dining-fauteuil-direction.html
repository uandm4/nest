story
In 1951 landmark French architect Jean Prouve brought his passion for industrial design into the home with the Vitra Fauteuil Direction Armchair.
Accentuating Prouve's philosophical aim of focusing on design factors, the Vitra Fauteuil Direction Armchair is perfect for a number of scenarios from the kitchen to the office, the latter environment in which it makes for a particularly neat combination with the Compas Direction Desk. Additionally, the Vitra Fauteuil Direction Armchair looks great in a number of other situations, such as lobby spaces, restaurants or waiting areas, and is complemented by the wider Fauteuil range.
Dimensions
Height: 81.5cm x Width: 65cm x Depth: 62.5cm
Seat height: 50.5cm
Materials & finishes
Base in powder-coated round and moulded sheet steel. Seat and backrest in polyurethane foam with covers in Vitra Twill fabric (new wool: 70%, polyamide: 30%). Armrests in natural or smoked oiled oak depending on finish. Completed with felt glides for hard floor use.

Jean Prouvé
Jean Prouve was born into an artistic family in Nancy, France. Prouve trained as a metalsmith before attending engineering school in Nancy, and his intimate knowledge of metal remained the foundation of his work and career.
After opening his own workshop in 1923, Prouve began producing modern metal furniture of his own design as well as collaborating with some of the best-known French designers of the day, including Le Corbusier and Charlotte Perriand.

story
An archetypal lamp, The Wastberg Chipperfield w102 Table Lamp offers a fresh interpretation of the familiar desk lamp, expertly designed by David Chipperfield.
With the intention to create a simple form without any visible joints but with a clear distinction between base, arm and light source. The w102 Table Lamp offers directional movement, without compromising its intended function. A personal desk lamp, available in a choice of solid brass or blackened steel this elegantly minimal design adds a touch of style to an array of contemporary interiors. Ideally utilised in a home study or at the office the Chipperfield w102 Table Lamp provides perfect task lighting, directed exactly where the user desires.

David Chipperfield
Born in 1953 in London, Sir David Chipperfield later studied at the Kingston School of Art and the Architectural Association in London.
After graduating, he established his own architectural practice in the early 1980s and went on to receive a multitude of awards and titles. These included becoming an Honorary Member of the Florence Academy of Art and Design, a Royal Designer for Industry (RDI) and receiving the Order of Merit of the Federal Republic of Germany.
One of his greatest successes was receiving the RIBA Royal Gold Medal for Architecture in 2011. Given in recognition of a lifetime’s work, the Royal Gold Medal is approved personally by Her Majesty the Queen.
